OLL -- Orient Last Layer

Orient all yellow pieces on the top face. Start with 2-look OLL (edges first, then corners) using just a few algorithms.

When do I do this?

After the first two layers (cross + four F2L pairs) are complete. The bottom two layers are fully solved, and you're now working only on the yellow (top) layer. The goal is to make the entire top face yellow -- the pieces don't need to be in the right positions yet, just oriented so yellow faces up.

2-Look OLL: How it works

Instead of learning all 57 OLL cases, beginners use two steps:

  1. Step 1 -- Orient edges: Look at the top face. You'll see a dot (no edges yellow), an L-shape (two adjacent edges yellow), or a line (two opposite edges yellow). Use the matching algorithm to make a yellow cross on top. If you already have a cross, skip to step 2.
  2. Step 2 -- Orient corners: You now have a yellow cross. Look at how many corners have yellow on top (0, 1, or 2). Match the pattern and apply the right algorithm. You may need to rotate the top layer (U turns) so the pattern lines up before applying.

How do I recognize the pattern?

Hold the cube so you can see the top face. The thumbnail on each card shows which positions should be yellow. Rotate the top layer (U moves) until your cube matches a thumbnail, then apply that algorithm.
