PLL -- Permute Last Layer

Permute all last layer pieces to their correct positions and solve the cube. Start with 2-look PLL (corners first, then edges).

When do I do this?

After OLL is complete. The entire top face is yellow, but the side colors of the top layer pieces are likely in the wrong positions. PLL rearranges them without disturbing the yellow orientation, completing the solve.

2-Look PLL: How it works

Instead of learning all 21 PLL cases, beginners use two steps:

  1. Step 1 -- Permute corners: Look at the side colors of the four top-layer corners. Check if any pair of adjacent corners have matching side colors ("headlights"). If you find one matching pair, hold them at the back and use a corner-swap algorithm. If no pair matches, apply the Y-perm from any angle to fix the diagonals.
  2. Step 2 -- Permute edges: The corners are now all correct. Look at the edges. Either three edges need to cycle (Ua or Ub perm, depending on the cycle direction), or two pairs of opposite edges need to swap (H-perm). If only one edge is wrong, do a U turn to check again.

How do I recognize the pattern?

For corners: rotate the top layer until you find a side where both top corners match each other's color. These are "headlights." If headlights exist, hold them at the back. For edges: after corners are solved, look at which edges need to move and in which direction.
