Cross

The first step of CFOP: solve four white edge pieces on the bottom face, aligned with their matching center colors. Aim for 8 moves or fewer.

When do I do this?

The cross is always the very first thing you solve on a scrambled cube. Hold the cube with the white center on the bottom. Your goal is to place all four white edge pieces on the bottom face so that each edge's second color also matches the adjacent center (e.g., the white-red edge sits between the white and red centers).

How do I recognize what to do?

Look at where each white edge currently is. It could be in the top layer, middle layer, or bottom layer, and it might be flipped. Each situation below shows a common case and the moves to fix it. The cross is mostly intuitive -- these examples teach you the patterns, but with practice you'll plan the entire cross during inspection.
